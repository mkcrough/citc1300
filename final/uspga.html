About The U.S. PGA Champions

U.S. PGA Champions Also called The PGA Championship or U.S. PGA. The tournament is organized by the Professional Golfers" Association of America. The first edition took place in 1916 in Siwanoy Country Club in Bronxville. It was won by Jim Barnes, who received 500 $ reward and a golden medal with brilliants. Later, the laureates received a trophy founded by Rodman Wanamaker who became the symbol of U.S. PGA. The tournament is dedicated to professional players who meet very demanding criteria. Fans can admire the highest level rivalry and sophisticated technique. Winning U.S. PGA guarantees numerous privileges including taking part in other three Grand Slam tournaments for the next five years and lifetime possibility to participate in U.S. PGA. The most titled players are Walter Hagen and Jack Nicklaus who won five editions and Tiger Woods, who won the championship four times.
